# Scripting Rules

Claude owns Stage 2 by default.

## Audience Framing — Business Owners (applies to every script)

Every script — foundation explainers included — is written for a business owner asking "what does this mean for my business," never for developers or hobbyists.

- Lead with the business stake (time/money/work removed), then the mechanics.
- Analogies and examples come from running a business, not from coding/hobby projects.
- Translate every concept into a business outcome in the same beat it appears.
- Tone is peer operator working AI out alongside them — not instructor to student.
- Tie every payoff to a business use case; never frame around fun/hobby use.

## Format — Required on Every Script

Every script must include:
- **YAML frontmatter**: voice_reference, script_type, hook_type, episode, series, target_words, target_length, draft_date, status
- **Production legend**: `*[Screen: ...]*` = visual cue; TH/LIVE tags; delivery note colour explained
- **TH/LIVE tag** at the top of every beat header
- **Word count target** beside every beat header
- **Voice checklist** at end — verified against the draft before saving
- **Read-through notes** for Daniel — delivery cues, pacing flags, optional additions

Output: PDF only. Write HTML → Chrome headless → PDF → delete HTML.
File naming: `{project_id}_script.pdf` inside `Youtube/Output/2. Scripts/{project_id}/`.

## Hook Rules

- Under 30 words for concept/tutorial videos; under 40 for story/build videos
- Never deliver value in the hook — open the gap, don't close it
- Hook types: Curiosity-Gap, Problem-Statement, Demonstration, Bold-Claim
- No intro, no "hey guys", no channel name — straight into it

---

## Credential Rules

- **Tutorial/concept videos**: NO credential drop. Skip entirely.
- **Story/build/business videos**: Anti-credential credential only — frame it as being on the journey, not having arrived. "I haven't built a successful AI business yet — that's the whole point."
- Never lead with achievements or social proof upfront

---

## Structural Rules

1. **Value-before-demo**: justify the tool or concept before showing it — prevent "so what?" during tutorials
2. **Count before enumeration**: always say "there are three things" before listing — never enumerate without announcing the count first
3. **Framework at WRAP**: the named framework (3 words or fewer) lands at the end, never front-loaded in hook or promise
4. **Channel angle as consequence**: Daniel's angle/premise should emerge from context — never declared upfront as "on this channel I..."
5. **Personal pivot in use-case sections**: always end a list of use cases with "in my case" or "that's how I landed on X"
6. **Thesis line technique**: the most important line in the video is never announced. It arrives naturally from context. Annotate in script: "Slow down. This is the line."

---

## Voice Rules

- **Gear-change transition**: use "Alright so —" to move between beats
- **Instruction style**: "you're gonna" — second person present tense — never "I will show you"
- **Natural imperfections**: include deliberate "um" or "uh" in scripted sections — do not clean them out
- **Small win celebrations**: "there we go" / "boom" — casual, never dramatic
- **Philosophy moments**: do not script them — annotate a moment where it could surface, let it come out naturally on camera
- **Self-aware time breaks**: when recording jumps forward in time, acknowledge it directly — no pretense of seamless continuity
- **Errors in demos**: keep them in — normalize with "you'll bump into stuff like this"
- **Verbatim AI prompts**: always show the exact prompt text when demonstrating AI use, not a summary

---

## Specificity Rules

- Real numbers always — "502 videos" not "about 500"
- Real tool names — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, n8n — never "an AI tool"
- No version numbers unless they matter to the demo
- Real outcomes, not hypothetical scenarios

---

## Vocabulary Rules

**Translate immediately on first use** — same sentence, not a footnote:
- Token → "the unit of text the model actually reads — roughly 4 characters per token in English"
- Context window → "how much text the model can hold in working memory at once"
- Temperature → "how adventurous or conservative the model is when choosing the next word"
- Fine-tuning → "training on human feedback to shape the model's personality"
- RLHF → avoid; say "trained on human feedback"
- Transformer → avoid; say "the architecture all these models share"

**Never use**: "Furthermore," "However," "It's worth noting that," "In conclusion," "Additionally"
**Always use**: "you're gonna see," "here's what's actually happening," "that's it," "alright so"

**Daniel's confirmed natural phrases — use these where they fit:**
- "And boom — done" — casual demo close, not dramatic
- "There it is" — result reveal, understated
- "Pretty cool, right?" — genuine check to viewer, not rhetorical
- "And I thought — right, [next thing]" — spoken internal reasoning
- "You just tell it what you want and it does it" — outcome-first summary
- "It learns" / "noticeably faster the second time" — normalises AI iteration
- "That's exactly what a new hire does" — go-to analogy for AI mistakes
- "The channel's just getting started" — honest subscribe lean-in, not performative

**Voice prompt demos:** When Daniel demonstrates AI on mobile, he uses his voice — not typing. Always script verbatim what he'll say in quotation marks so he can read it directly.

## Analogy Rules

- At least 2 analogies per concept video — density is HIGH, not decorative
- Each analogy gets a label in the script (musician analogy, chef analogy, phone keyboard analogy)
- Plain English immediately after every technical term before moving on
- Analogies live in the body — not saved for the wrap

## CTA — Locked Template

"If you got something out of this, give it a like — it genuinely helps. Next video is [title] — specifically [one-line tease]. If you want that one, subscribe. See you in the next one."

Must include: like ask → next video tease → subscribe. In that order.

## Pacing Targets by Video Type

**Concept/explainer** (~1,500w for 10 min, ~900w for 6 min):
Hook 2% | Promise 5% | Body beats 75–80% | Wrap 5% | CTA 3%

**Tutorial/build** (~1,800–2,100w for 12–14 min):
Hook 2% | Promise 3% | Credential 3% | Demo sections 80% | Wrap + Framework 7% | CTA 3%

**Story/business** (~1,200–1,500w for 8–10 min):
Hook 3% | Promise 4% | Credential 5% | Use cases (3) 60% | Personal pivot 8% | Wrap + Framework 12% | CTA 4%

Do NOT generate for:
- Pure demo or tutorial videos where the screen recording carries all visual weight
- Talking-head-only videos with no slides confirmed in Phase 1

## Review Rules

- Use AskUserQuestion panel tool for ALL interview and review questions — never ask in plain chat text
- During Phase 1 and Phase 2: group up to 4 questions per panel call; use multiple choice options (2–4) where possible

**Phase 4 review — strict beat-by-beat flow:**
1. Paste the beat's script text in the chat (never inside the panel options — the panel is too small)
2. Immediately open one AskUserQuestion panel for that beat only
3. Wait for Daniel to respond
4. If changes are needed: propose the specific edit in chat, then open a follow-up panel to confirm
5. Only when Daniel explicitly confirms the beat: move to the next beat in sequence
6. Never jump ahead, never go back to a previous beat, never group multiple beats into one review panel
7. Review order is always Beat 1 → Beat 2 → Beat 3 → ... in strict sequence — no exceptions
8. Never skip a beat, even ones that seem likely to be approved quickly
9. Do not generate Draft 2 until every beat has been confirmed in sequence
10. Propose specific edits based on feedback — never ask open-ended "what should I change?"
11. If Daniel says "fine" or approves without engaging on a beat that reads weak, push back once with a specific question before moving on

## Description YAML Rules

**When:** Phase 7 — triggered by the `/description` command, or (future) automatically by a file watcher when an SRT or transcript file lands in `Youtube/Output/6. Review  /{project_id}/`. Not part of the Phase 1–6 scripting session.

**File naming:** Use the canonical project prefix.
- Video: `{project_id}_review.mp4` → YAML: `{project_id}_metadata.yaml`
- Always `.yaml` — never `.yml`

**Save to:** `Youtube/Output/6. Review  /{project_id}/` — the review agent prepares the exact publishing pair.

**Required fields:**

| Field | Rule |
|-------|------|
| `title` | Crafted from video content (never a placeholder). Max 100 chars. Lead with specificity — real numbers, real outcome. |
| `description` | Multi-line block. Max 5,000 chars. See writing rules below. |
| `tags` | Omit entirely — do not include a tags field in any YAML file. |
| `category_id` | Always `"28"` (Science & Technology). |
| `made_for_kids` | Always `false`. |

**Description writing rules:**

1. **First 150 chars are critical** — appear in search and YouTube previews. Must be a curiosity gap or bold claim pulled directly from the video hook. Never generic ("In this video I show you…").
2. **Body** (2–3 sentences): outcome-first. What the viewer walks away knowing or able to do. Use real numbers from the video — never round them.
3. **TOOLS USED section** (tutorials/demos) or **LINKS MENTIONED** (story/business): only tools, platforms, or resources actually named in the video. Include the URL where known (e.g. `github.com/yt-dlp/yt-dlp`). No invented links.
4. **Skool community link**: not available yet. Omit until Daniel provides it — do not add placeholder text to the live description.
5. **TIMESTAMPS section**: built from the SRT file in Review. Format: `M:SS – {Section Name}`. No leading zeros on minutes. First entry always `0:00 –`. **Chapter count scales with video length** — for a ~10 min video use no more than 5 timestamps; for shorter videos use fewer. Never exceed 5 for anything under 15 minutes. Pick only the major topic shifts — not every sub-step. **Every chapter must be at least 60 seconds from the previous one.** If two candidates are fewer than 60 seconds apart, drop the less significant one. YouTube silently rejects the entire chapter set if any chapter is under 10 seconds; a 60-second floor keeps chapters well clear of that and produces a clean, readable chapter list.
6. **Hashtags**: 8–12 at the end of the description string. Order: broad → niche. These are inside the `description` field — not in the `tags` list.
7. **No `tags` field**: omit entirely from every YAML file — do not include it.

**Forbidden in descriptions:**
- "In this video…" as an opener
- Generic openers that don't mirror the actual hook
- Made-up links or placeholder URLs
- Hashtags in the `tags` list
