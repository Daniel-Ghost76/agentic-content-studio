## Creator Signatures

### Nate Herk
- **Background:** Dark (black, charcoal, dark gray). Almost never bright.
- **Accent colors:** Orange `#E85000` · Teal `#00B8CC` · White text
- **Signature element:** Teal pill/capsule callout box around a key word ("EVERYTHING", "24/7", "Both", "View")
- **Face:** Right side of frame. Smiling or excited. Same sweater/casual look.
- **Props:** Orange Anthropic asterisk embedded in text as letter replacement ("st*p", "sh*t")
- **Layout default:** Face right + terminal/UI screenshot or app icons left, pointing gesture toward the element
- **Text style:** Heavy bold sans-serif, uppercase, top or bottom anchor, orange highlights numbers

### Liam Ottley
- **Background:** Two modes — (1) Da Vinci notebook paper (off-white sketch texture) for most content; (2) outdoors/lifestyle for personal/opinion content
- **Accent colors:** Orange + Blue for contrast pairs · Yellow for shock/urgency · Black for dark-mode
- **Signature element:** Bottom orange bar with white bold caps ("AUTOMATE YOUR BUSINESS", "THIS RUNS MY BUSINESS")
- **Face:** Left or center. Wide-eyed shock or serious stare for viral content. Smiling for tutorial content.
- **Props:** 3D-rendered app icons (glossy, floating) · Robot arms · Illustrated cartoon mascots
- **Layout default:** Face left + text/icon right OR full-screen lifestyle shot with large overlay text
- **Text style:** Very heavy, often sentence-case for lifestyle ("EVERYTHING'S CHANGED", "YOUR MOVE"), pill labels for comparisons

### Nick Saraev
- **Background:** Two modes — (1) Warm beige/cream for clean/tutorial content; (2) Black/dark for competitive/viral content
- **Accent colors:** Claude orange `#E07050` on everything · Black for text on light bg · White on dark bg
- **Signature element:** Mac window chrome (red/yellow/green dots) framing a `/command` or Claude logo — extremely distinctive
- **Face:** Right side when face is included. Warm smile. Used selectively — ~half of thumbnails are no-face.
- **Props:** Claude asterisk logo (in nearly every thumbnail) · Orange curved arrows · Red X over competitor icons
- **Layout default:** Mac window left + face right, OR icon-only composition (no face) with declarative text
- **Text style:** Slash commands (`/skills`, `/free`, `/hacked`) as primary anchor. Declarative sentences ending with period ("It's too good.", "Total victory."). Pixel font for competitive content.

## Layout Archetypes (ordered by versatility)

### 1. FACE-RIGHT + MOCKUP-LEFT
**Best for:** Tutorial, tool demo, feature reveal, course  
**Structure:** Face occupies right 40–50% of frame. UI screenshot, app icon cluster, or whiteboard diagram fills left. Pointing gesture or arrow directs attention. Text at top or bottom.  
**Examples:** Nate — `jZgcWCzxh1I` (workflow terminal), `ZAaxx3qyT8g` (agent view). Nick — `K65vd9EYbDU` ($1M app), `UPtmKh1vMN8` (course terminal). Liam — `_He58Zi8NQg` (phone).  
**Hook trigger:** "What is this thing he's showing me?"

### 2. PILL/BAR CALLOUT + FACE
**Best for:** New feature drop, counter-intuitive take, "here's the thing"  
**Structure:** 2–4 word lead text (white, heavy) + one key word inside a colored pill (teal, orange, blue). Face to the right. No cluttered background.  
**Examples:** Nate — `ZAaxx3qyT8g` ("Agent View" teal), `xJ5oz63mIec` ("24/7" teal), `kB9iMD0EjT8` ("Both" teal). Liam — `NlGIl5-gpHM` (orange bar "EVERYTHING").  
**Hook trigger:** The pill makes one word feel like a reveal the viewer almost missed.

### 3. COMPARISON SPLIT
**Best for:** Old vs new, tool vs tool, before/after, then vs now  
**Structure:** Two distinct visual halves (or panels) with contrast labels — colored pills, dates, or descriptors (OLD/NEW, ZERO→AIOS, 2007→2026). Face optional in center. Red X or green circle as verdict.  
**Examples:** Nate — `RLjaUES9P8A` (Claude Code vs Codex). Liam — `KAufsCas_F4` (SaaS vs Skill.md), `qNtDxuLfFE8` (2007→2026). Nick — `EsTrWCV0Ph4` (ZERO TO HERO + platforms list).  
**Hook trigger:** Forces a binary choice — viewer needs to know which side wins.

### 4. SLASH COMMAND / CLEAN ICON CARD
**Best for:** Specific tool, Claude feature, course, workflow  
**Structure:** Mac window chrome frames a large `/command` or tool name with Claude logo. Warm beige or dark background. Face right (optional). Orange arrow pointing at key detail.  
**Examples:** Nick — `j3aXJNu9804` (/routines), `sduaTkhIm_w` (/skills), `U6gg_bi1I70` (/free), `2u93VTYvG5U` (/hacked), `BMMcmmnjrM8` (/mobile).  
**Hook trigger:** Insider vocabulary — signals the viewer will learn something precise and actionable.

### 5. LARGE DECLARATIVE TEXT
**Best for:** Opinion, reaction, competitive take, news response  
**Structure:** 3–5 word sentence as full thumbnail anchor. Ends with period for finality. White background (Nick) or dark (Liam). Minimal supporting imagery (benchmark table, icon pair). No face required.  
**Examples:** Nick — `oCuttuCQmZg` ("It's too good."), `ot3NM5OVFmc` ("Total victory."), `NF10evwkefM` ("No more OpenClaw."), `ZbKNpn0woDY` ("Save 2x tokens."). Liam — `GyCNs5AymRw` ("EVERYTHING'S CHANGED"), `8JLyq_-3n58` ("THE PATH IS CLEAR").  
**Hook trigger:** Confidence gap — viewer needs to know why you're so certain.

### 6. COMPETITIVE BATTLE (icon-only)
**Best for:** New model drop, tool comparison, Claude winning  
**Structure:** Winner icon (Claude, glowing) in center or left. Competitor icons (cracked, crying, red X) flanking. No face. Title text above. Dark or white background.  
**Examples:** Nick — `Ob5Vu-gD3mo` (animated battle), `WVQ0lPiWsHQ` (Opus dominates), `NF10evwkefM` (Claude vs OpenClaw X).  
**Hook trigger:** Pure tribal loyalty — viewer roots for their chosen tool.

### 7. WHITEBOARD / DIAGRAM CONCEPT
**Best for:** Framework explanation, decision-making, "how this works"  
**Structure:** Creator stands beside a whiteboard or screen showing a diagram, red X's on wrong options, green circle on right answer, arrows at a fork in the road.  
**Examples:** Nate — `Pi-m8R068r4` (AI OS whiteboard), `iIfOprq2kCM` (AI agency fork). Liam — `qJg3CuqEvek` (illustrated character), `2bsfQThGXxc` (robot arms + checklist).  
**Hook trigger:** Simplification promise — "he'll make this complicated thing clear."

### 8. MULTI-PANEL TIMELINE / LEVELS
**Best for:** History explainer, skill levels, options comparison  
**Structure:** 2–4 panels side-by-side, each with a label (era, level, category). Distinct visual per panel (photo, icon, screenshot). Labels at top. Face not required.  
**Examples:** Nate — `NHFbAg2b54U` (1950/2020/NOW), `ZRb7D6R64hM` (BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE/ADVANCED). Liam — `oC1h922cDoY` (1800s/1990s/NOW), `CSULpfABy0I` (4 business models).  
**Hook trigger:** Progress/status anxiety — "which panel am I in right now?"

### 9. TWEET / PROOF SCREENSHOT + REACTION
**Best for:** Breaking news, industry moment, credibility event  
**Structure:** Real tweet or benchmark screenshot (with neon glow border — blue or orange) occupying left/center. Face reacting right (smiling, shocked, pointing). Text overlay if needed.  
**Examples:** Nate — `-nG-9vlSkho` (Sam Altman + Claude Devs tweets). Nick — `4Cb_l2LJAW8` (Karpathy GitHub repo).  
**Hook trigger:** "This is real, and he knows how to interpret it for me."

### 10. LIFESTYLE / ENVIRONMENT SHOT
**Best for:** Personal opinion, life update, aspirational content  
**Structure:** Creator in outdoor or lifestyle setting (pool, rooftop, mountain). Full or 3/4 body shot. 2–4 word text overlay at top, heavy and uppercase. No props or mockups.  
**Examples:** Liam — `ykRToEkWvpA` ("YOUR MOVE" poolside), `GyCNs5AymRw` ("EVERYTHING'S CHANGED" pool), `8JLyq_-3n58` ("THE PATH IS CLEAR" mountain).  
**Hook trigger:** Aspiration + urgency. "He has the life — what does he know that I don't?"

## Text Formula Bank

**Curiosity gap (opens a loop)**
- `It [Verb]s [Everything/Everyone]`
- `[Tool] Just Changed [Everything/The Rules]`
- `Nobody Is Talking About [This]`
- `I Tested [X] — Here's [What Happened/The Truth]`
- `[Big Number] [Outcome]` (e.g. "93,000,000 Tokens Saved")

**Contrast / Comparison**
- `[OLD] vs [NEW]` / `[ZERO] → [HERO]`
- `Stop [Wrong Thing]. Do [This].`
- `[X] is Dead. Do [Y].`
- `[Year] vs [Year]` / `Then vs Now`
- `[Option A] × [Option B] ✓`

**Authority / Outcome**
- `[Number] [Units] in [Time]` (e.g. "$1M/yr in rev")
- `[Credential Drop]: [Verdict]`
- `[Bold Claim].` (declarative, ends with period)
- `Only [Small %] [Achieve This]`

**Directive / Action**
- `[Verb] [This]` — `Copy This`, `Use Both`, `Do This Now`
- `Just [Verb] [Thing]` — `Just Use These`
- `Your [Noun]` — `Your Move`
- `/[slash-command]` — insider shorthand

**Urgency / FOMO**
- `[X] Is Making You [Negative Outcome]`
- `[Opportunity] Nobody Is Talking About`
- `Before [Deadline/Event], [Do This]`
- `[X] is Here. [Implication].`

## Face Placement Rules

| Placement | When to use |
|-----------|-------------|
| Face right (default) | Mockup/diagram/UI on left. Right-facing is most natural for left-to-right reading flow. |
| Face center | Competitive split (two things flanking), or feature-card fan layout. |
| Face left | When the key visual element is on the right and needs right-side reading priority. |
| No face | Icon-only battles, slash-command cards, timeline infographics, benchmark screenshots. |

**Expression guidance:**
- Smiling/open → tutorial, tool reveal, "here's the good news"
- Shocked/wide-eyed → news drop, unexpected result, aspiration
- Serious/neutral → opinion, verdict, personal take
- Pointing → always toward the key visual element

**Face treatment:**
- No heavy glow or cutout needed if background is dark enough to separate
- Light hair-glow acceptable for light backgrounds to separate head from background
- No border frames around the face — let it breathe

## Supporting Element Library

**App icon treatment:**
- Flat icon: clean white/light backgrounds (Nick style)
- 3D glossy icon: dark or textured backgrounds (Liam style, Nate style)
- Glowing/neon icon: competitive battle thumbnails only

**Arrows:**
- Orange curved arrow: pointing to detail in UI (Nick signature)
- White straight arrow with label: comparison directionality (ZERO→HERO)
- Hand pointing (in-frame gesture): preferred over graphic arrows when face is present

**Verdict markers:**
- Red X: loser, wrong approach, old way
- Green circle/oval: winner, right approach, endorsed option
- Strikethrough: deprecated tool or wrong approach written out

**UI mockup types (ranked by impact):**
1. Mac terminal / dark code window — signals technical authority
2. Mac window chrome (red/yellow/green dots) — signals Claude workflow
3. Phone held up showing app — signals mobile/accessible
4. Laptop with app dashboard — signals real results / revenue
5. Whiteboard diagram — signals explanation / simplification
6. Tweet / X post — signals news credibility

## Scoring Rubric

Score each concept 1–10 on:

| Dimension | What it means |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Curiosity** | Does the viewer feel they NEED to know what happens in this video? |
| **Clarity** | Can you understand the topic in under 1 second? |
| **Emotional punch** | Does it trigger a feeling (excitement, FOMO, curiosity, aspiration)? |
| **Phone readability** | Is the text and key element visible at 200px wide (phone grid size)? |

Recommended concept = highest combined score. Minimum threshold: average of 7/10 across all four.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Generic AI glow** — purple/blue aura around a logo with no specific claim. Zero of these 50 thumbnails use it. Skip it entirely.
- **Text over 5 words** — all three channels cap their primary hook at 2–5 words. Longer text fails at phone size.
- **Thin or light font weight** — every channel uses heavy/black weight fonts. Light fonts disappear.
- **Face too small** — if face is included, it should fill at least 35% of the frame height. Tiny face = no emotional connection.
- **Cluttered background** — busy textures behind a busy UI behind text. Everything competes. Hierarchy breaks.
- **Abstract concept with no concrete anchor** — "AI Future" with a galaxy. Nothing to latch onto. Every strong thumbnail has at least one: a number, a tool logo, a real screenshot, or a real face.
- **Copying exact Nate Herk layouts** — don't import his teal pill verbatim. Use the principle (key word gets a callout box) but make it Daniel's own color system.

## Daniel's Channel — Application Notes

Daniel's channel targets the same audience (AI builders, Claude Code users, small business owners automating with AI). The visual language should borrow from all three channels but establish its own signature:

- **Own color:** Daniel needs a consistent accent color that isn't identical to any of the three references. Orange is already Claude's color — use it as an AI context signal, not as a personal brand color. Consider a second accent (e.g. electric green, deep purple, or bright yellow) as Daniel's personal signature.
- **Face position:** Face right is the safest default given Daniel's content type (tutorials, Claude Code, workflow builds).
- **Text anchor:** Pick ONE text pattern as Daniel's signature and repeat it. Nick's period-ending declarations work well for authority. Nate's teal pill works for feature reveals. Choose one.
- **Background:** Dark is safer for YouTube CTR in this niche — Nate and Nick both use it for competitive content.
- **UI element:** Always include a real Claude Code terminal, app interface, or production output when possible. Abstract "AI" visuals underperform.
