## What It Is

A Higgsfield-generated video opening shot. A grid of floating, softly blurred screenshot panels arranged in the background (suggesting "I've done the research / I've used the tool a lot"), with a centred dark glass card in the foreground displaying a product name, icon, and a progress or metric element. Used as the first full-screen visual before the presenter appears — establishes credibility and hook.

## When To Use

- Video opening, before or immediately after the hook (first 5–10 seconds)
- Any video where the premise is "I spent X hours with this tool" or "I've tested X things"
- As an establishing shot that signals volume of work done

## When Not To Use

- Mid-video — this is an opening shot only
- When the video is not about a specific tool or product
- Back to back with another full-screen clip — needs breathing room

## What Good Looks Like

- Background: `~8–12` small screenshot/window panels arranged in an asymmetric grid, soft gaussian blur, low opacity (~25–40%)
- Foreground center card: dark glass, `~900px wide`, rounded corners, containing:
  - Product logo icon (left, ~100px)
  - Product name in white (center-right, ~48–56px bold)
  - A horizontal progress bar or metric beneath the name (e.g. "0hrs → 400hrs")
- Overall palette: near-black background, teal/cool accent glow at top edge of center card
- Slow atmospheric drift on the background panels (parallax feel, not spinning)
- Duration: 4–6 seconds, settles cleanly
