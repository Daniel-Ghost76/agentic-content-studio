# 05c — Quote Highlight

**Sub-library:** `05_emphasis_word_overlays`  
**Status:** draft

## What It Is

A short sentence or phrase that builds in word-by-word or line-by-line to emphasise a thesis, framework line, or memorable quote. Not subtitles — this is a designed visual statement.

## When To Use

- Daniel delivers a thesis line he wants the viewer to remember
- A framework is being named and the name is a short sentence
- A memorable quote or principle is being introduced

## When Not To Use

- Subtitle replacement — use actual captions for that
- Sentences longer than 10 words
- More than once per major section of the video

## What Good Looks Like

- 1–2 lines, 36–52px, high-contrast white on transparent
- Words reveal left-to-right: each word fades/slides in at ~100ms intervals
- Optional: a subtle underline or marker sweep appears after the full phrase lands
- Short hold after full reveal (1.5–2.5s)
- Centred or slightly left-aligned if composited next to Daniel
