# 05b — Two-Word Contrast

**Sub-library:** `05_emphasis_word_overlays`  
**Status:** draft

## What It Is

Two words or short phrases displayed in visual contrast — one represents the old/bad/before state, the other the new/good/after state. Used for reframes, pivots, and before/after moments.

## When To Use

- Reframing a concept: "MANUAL → AUTOMATED", "SLOW → INSTANT", "COMPLEX → SIMPLE"
- Daniel makes a comparison and wants it to land visually
- A pivot moment in the narrative where one approach is replaced by another

## When Not To Use

- When the contrast is subtle or nuanced — this is bold, binary communication
- When more than two concepts are in play
- Decoratively — every use should have a clear rhetorical purpose

## What Good Looks Like

- Two short phrases, stacked or side-by-side
- Word A: dimmer/secondary colour (the old/bad state)
- Word B: brighter/primary or accent colour (the new/good state)
- Arrow or separator (thin `→` or `/`) between them
- Word A appears first, then separator, then Word B — sequential
- Transparent canvas, centred or slightly above centre
